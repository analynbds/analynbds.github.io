Penn Ten Campaignn

A microsite, photos, videos, and social media graphics, to highlight the movers and shakers at Penn.

My Role: Creative Director, UX Design for Websites

Introduction

The inaugural Penn Ten was a campaign by The Daily Pennsylvanian (DP) in 2015 to find and feature the “most impressive” undergraduates at the University of Pennsylvania. The alumni judges included Randall Lane, then-Editor of Forbes Magazine (current Chief Content Officer) and an active Daily Pennsylvanian alumnus. Thus this project was very much inspired by Forbes’ inaugural 30 Under 30 campaign in 2014. The campaign features a list of prominent and emerging leaders and entrepreneurs of various industries under 30 years old.


The Challenge

This project was one of the first company-wide projects sanctioned by the Innovation Director to create products outside the DP’s typical scope and to create more content and traffic for the newspaper.

Deliverables included branding collateral, a microsite, a group photo, profile shots and videos of each candidate. As Creative Director at the DP at the time, I was tasked to oversee and coordinate with my editors the production and timeliness of the project. As an aspiring digital designer, I wanted to help design and build the microsite.


Research

Since this project was inspired by Forbes’ 30 Under 30 campaign, I drew a lot of inspiration that. The Innovation Director, Taylor Culliver, was working at Forbes at the time, and I had the opportunity to volunteer at the first Under 30 Summit and do a bit of field work.

The Food Festival event on the last night of the 30 Under 30 Summit on October 21, 2014 in Philadelphia, PA.

Since 30 Under 30’s target audience is millennials, the entire campaign was very energetic and lively. It culminated with the Food Festival and a night-club party right afterwards. Taking these things into consideration, we decided that Penn Ten should also be lively and cool yet prestigious for the Penn audience.

I encouraged the Photo and Video departments to get footage that represented these themes. Their staff should shadow the candidates and capture intimate shots of each candidate showcasing his/her work and personality.


The Outcome


Logo


Social Media Graphics

To optimize Facebook sharing, I made sure the following graphics appeared as thumbnails on Facebook by using Open Graph tags (property="og:image" ). The graphics featured photos that best demonstrated the candidates and quotes with the ubiquitous blue circle element to grab people's’ attention and denote that these stories were part of the Penn Ten series.


Microsite

I ended up building each individual candidate page in Adobe Muse. I added parallax animation and full-width media to create movement and dynamism.

Results

The website received hundreds of shares and likes on Facebook as the candidates shared the website and their respective articles with their friends and family.

The microsite also won 5th place in the Multimedia Feature of the Year category at the Associated Collegiate Press National Awards in 2015.

The project also allowed the creative editors and their staff to tackle on photographing and filming more in-depth stories and expand their skills and passions.
