Drive Electric Hawaii Website

Building a website to educate Hawaii about electric vehicles.

My Role: UX Research and Visual Design

Intro

Drive Electric Hawaii is a website created by Anthology Marketing Group in Honolulu for a coalition of 8 organizations comprising of Blue Planet Foundation; Hawai‘i State Department of Transportation (HDOT); Hawaii State Department of Business, Economic Development and Tourism (DBEDT); Hawaii State Division of Consumer Advocacy; the Hawaiian Electric Companies (including Maui Electric and Hawai‘i Electric Light); Kauai Island Utility Cooperative; Rocky Mountain Institute; and Ulupono Initiative. While interning at Anthology, I was given the opportunity to help design site, specifically on the visual design side.

The Callenge

We were tasked with creating a “fun” yet informational site that appealed to a broad range of people in Hawaii, specifically focusing on those from age 20-40 years old. To incorporate the idea of “fun,” our lead designer thought it would be cool to have a car “drive” through scenes of information to entice visitors into the site and learn more.

As mentioned, I got the opportunity to come up with quite a bit of the branding and the look and feel of the site, including creating mood boards, icons, and deciding font choices. Since this website was a completely new product and had no existing collateral, I was tasked with researching possible concepts for the site.

The project had a relatively quick turnaround time of a little over a month.

Research

The client wanted the site to be “fun,” which in design-terms can mean a slew of things... Fortunately we were provided a few existing sites to look at, including ElectroMotiveLA, Utah Clean Cities, and Go Forth. We were also inspired by the booming downtown Honolulu scene and the growth going on there, as well as the energetic nature of the outer islands of Hawaii.

Instead of describing the site as “fun” alone, we added some Vitality, Energy, Modernity, and Freshness into the site.

Visual Design

After doing the research, I compiled a mood board of photos to encapsulate the feelings above. The color palette, which was compiled by a fellow designer on the team, drew upon these colors, and included vibrant hues that represented nature, city-life, and dawn. Gradients were also implemented in the design for the modern aspect.

Also, because we wanted to focus on modernity, for the iconography, I thought it would be fun to do those trendy line/ghost style.

Outcome

See the live site here: DriveElectricHI.com

Reflection

Even though the turnaround time was super quick, we were able to get the website done on time because of the collaborative efforts of the team at Anthology. It was definitely fun to explore the visual design side of Experience Design, and after this project I was quite excited to learn more about the information architecture side of things as well.
